Dr. Semmelweis and the Importance of Handwashing

I completed a data analysis project on Dr. Semmelweis’ discovery of handwashing and its impact on reducing maternal mortality. Using R and ggplot2, I explored historical hospital records to analyze how implementing handwashing influenced death rates.

Key tasks and insights from the project:

✔ Data Wrangling: Loaded and structured yearly and monthly mortality data from CSV files.

✔ Proportion of Deaths Analysis: Calculated the proportion of deaths per births for each year and month to observe trends.

✔ Visualization with ggplot2: Created a yearly line plot, distinguishing mortality trends between two clinics. Designed a monthly trend plot, adding a marker for when handwashing was introduced (June 1st, 1847).

✔ Impact of Handwashing: Added a handwashing_started column to categorize data before and after June 1847. Compared the mean proportion of deaths before and after handwashing, showing a significant decline in mortality rates.

This project reinforced my expertise in data visualization, statistical analysis, and storytelling with R, while also highlighting the power of data-driven decision-making in Public health.
